The Process

  1. Your group is responsible for choosing 3 of the figurative language types below to create the PowerPoint that you will use to teach the incarcerated graffiti artists. You will need to research each of the three types and create a definition, show some examples of what that type of figurative language looks like, and then come up with an activity for the incarcerated artists to complete that will help them practice using the figurative language correctly.
    • Metaphor
    • Simile
    • Onomatopoeia
    • Personification
    • Alliteration
    • Hyperbole
    • Idiom
    • Oxymoron
    • Assonance
  2. Each member in your group has an important role to play. Take a few minutes to discuss these 4 roles and then determine who in your group will be filling each role. In addition to fulfilling the duties of your role, you will also be helping your other group members accomplish their tasks. Remember: THIS IS A GROUP PROJECT!
    • Chief Recorder - responsible for filling in graphic organizer with information from websites and group discussions.
    • Chief Example Generator - responsible for creating examples for the group's Figurative Language topics after group research and discussion.
    • Chief Navigator - responsible for controlling website use and navigating through the links to look through information.
    • Chief Designer - responsible for designing final PowerPoint layout, with input from other group members.
  3. You will now begin your research. Print out the attached graphic organizer so your recorder can copy down important information about the discussion you and your group members have about your chosen topics. Below is a list of websites you can use for your research.

  4. Use this template to begin creating your PowerPoint presentation. If you need help, click here for an example of a completed PowerPoint. This example lists only 1 type of figurative language, but yours MUST contain 3.
  5. Your group will need to come up with an activity that the class can work on that will help to reinforce the types of figurative language that you have taught during your presentation. You can have the students fill out a worksheet, play a game, or create something that demonstrates that they have understood and learned from your lesson. Make sure to include the details for the activity in your PowerPoint presentation.
