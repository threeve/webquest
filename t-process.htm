Process

The projected timeframe for this WebQuest is seven days considering that each class period is 44 minutes. However, some flexibility will be necessary depending upon whether students need extra time for research or if additional days need to be added due to the time required for each presentation and chosen group activity.

If the class does not divide equally into groups consisting of four members, some groups can have two Chief Recorders or Chief Example Generators.

The breakdown of the days is as follows:

Day One: Introduction and Group Assignments

Students will acquire from the teacher a basic understanding of WebQuests, their purpose, navigation, and layout. Students will also learn the members of their group for this project. A brief timeline will be provided by the teacher for group members to use as a pacing guide for the completion of this project.

The rest of the class period is time for groups to become familiar with the Figurative Language Webquest and assign individual roles to group members.

Day Two: Research

Students will form their groups and begin navigating through the provided web sources to decide their three figurative language types they wish to present to the class. They will also begin to fill out the provided graphic organizer and collaborate possible peer activities related to the three choice figurative language types.

Day Three: Research and Presentation Planning

Students will continue their research, construct their PowerPoint, and determine their activity.

Day Four: Finalizing the Presentation and Preparing Materials for Activity

Students will add the remaining components to their PowerPoint, decide who the speaker will be for each slide, and gather materials for their chosen activity.

Note - an additional day(s) may be needed to complete this process.

Day Five: Group Presentations

Three groups present their PowerPoint and activity.

Day Six: Group Presentations

Two groups present their PowerPoint and activity.

Day Seven: Group Presentations

The remaining groups present their PowerPoint and activity.

Any remaining time will be used for students to provide feedback to their learning via the Figurative Language WebQuest. Important items to be discussed include:

  • What did you like about the WebQuest?
  • What did you or your team struggle with during this process?
  • Did you enjoy learning from your peers?
  • What changes would you make to the WebQuest?
