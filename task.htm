The Task

Illiterate graffiti artists have caused mass confusion by littering the streets with mixed figurative language messages. They have been incarcerated and your job is to create a PowerPoint that will teach these young hooligans the proper use of figurative language so they can clean up their act.

 

The Criminals

Tom Foolery

His Crime: Mixed Metaphors and Similes

  • He's not the sharpest crayon in the shed.
  • It isn't rocket surgery.
  • She's not the toughest cookie on the block.
  • It's as plain as the ear on your face.
  • Life is like a box of cherries.
  • You must have ears like an eagle.

 

Silly Sam

His Crime: Idiotic Idioms

  • Actions speak louder than verbs.
  • That dog is all bite and no bark.
  • It costs an arm and an ear.
  • She's the apple of my knee.
  • He is a front seat driver.
  • Third time's a duck.

 

Bumbling Bubba

His Crime: Heinous Hyperboles

  • I'm so hungry I could eat a peanut.
  • He was so heavy the elevator would only go up.
  • She wears so much makeup that when she takes it off she gains 30 pounds!
  • I was so shocked you could have knocked me over with a tractor.
  • Her brain is the size of her head.
